Luminescence Thermometry - under construction Luminescent thermometry is a rapid expanding field with its grounds on the second half of last century. It uses the light emission from a system to probe the temperature of the surroundings. Of special interest is the use of single NCs toward the realization of temperature sensing in nanoscale with high spatial, temporal, and thermal resolution. In the past decades, advancements in the synthesis of Lanthanide (Ln3+)-doped nanocrystals (NC) have provided powerful tools for temperature sensing applications. Combining high accuracy, low toxicity, and high photostability, optical thermometers based on such systems are being exploited heavily by the scientific community nowadays. My work is to study the physics behind the temperature probing of single nanoparticles, and how the measuring process itself influences the temperature readouts.
